Workshop on Space-Air-Ground-Sea Integrated Sensing, Computing, and Communications For 6G IoT


In conjunction with the 2025 IEEE 101st Vehicular Technology Conference (VTC2025-Spring)

17 - 20 June 2025





THE WORKSHOP SCOPE

With the explosive growth of the Internet of Things (IoT) services and applications, such as autonomous driving, digital twins, blockchain, VR/AR, industry 4.0, etc. Currently, due to the limited traditional network capacity, the computation capacity limitation of the end user, and the long latency of the backhaul links, the traditional wireless communications systems' functionalities of mobile sensing, communications, and computation have been designed in isolation. Therefore, reducing the delay and energy consumption of the entire wireless communications system including all the above functionalities is still difficult. Meanwhile, there has been an exponential multiplication of communication systems and paradigms, with their scope enlarging beyond the traditional terrestrial domain to incorporate atmospheric, extraterrestrial, and subaqueous realms. The 6G era is poised to revolutionise the existing wireless communications networks through the synergistic integration of mobile sensing, communications and computation of the space-air-ground-sea (SAGS) multiple domains. Different from the existing 5G networks' capacity increase research, 6G is poised to bring novel distributed architectures according to the SAGS framework to achieve ultra-low latency, ultra-high reliability, and ultra-high-capacity requirements of different IoT applications and services. Participants will gain insight into the foundational principles, current trends, and future directions of 6G-empowered IoT, fostering a comprehensive understanding of their interplay and implications for various industries.

We are seeking original, completed, and unpublished works that are not under review elsewhere, aiming to assemble a collection of high-quality, crosscutting research papers on a variety of topics, which include, but are not limited to:

  • Mobile edge computing in space-air-ground-sea integrated networks for 6G IoT
  • AI-empowered space-air-ground-sea integrated deep connectivity for 6G IoT
  • Cloud/Edge Computing offloading for space-air-ground-sea integrated for 6G IoT
  • Network architectures/transmission protocols for space-air-ground-sea integrated for 6G IoT
  • Space-Air-Ground-Sea integrated radio frequency systems design for 6G IoT
  • Communication and computation resources management for space-air-ground-sea integrated for 6G IoT
  • Digital twin-empowered space-air-ground-sea integrated for 6G IoT
  • Blockchain-empowered space-air-ground-sea integrated for 6G IoT
  • Efficient mobility management in space-air-ground-sea integrated for 6G IoT
  • Joint communication, computing, and sensing in space-air-ground-sea integrated for 6G IoT
  • Federated/machine/deep learning-empowered in space-air-ground-sea integrated for 6G IoT
  • Federated/machine/deep learning-assisted 6G IoT communication, computing, and sensing.

INSTRUCTIONS FOR AUTHORS

Submitted manuscripts must be formatted in standard IEEE camera-ready format (double-column, 10-pt font) and must be submitted via link (TBD) as PDF files (formatted for 8.5x11-inch paper). The manuscripts should be no longer than 6 pages . Submitted papers cannot have been previously published in or be under consideration for publication in another journal or conference. The workshop program committee reserves the right to not review papers that either exceed the length specification or have been submitted or published elsewhere. Submissions must include a title, abstract, keywords, author(s) and affiliation(s) with postal and e-mail address(es).





REVIEW AND PUBLICATION OF MANUSCRIPTS

Submitted papers will be reviewed by the workshop program committee and judged on originality, technical correctness, relevance, and quality of presentation. An accepted paper must be presented at the IEEE VTC-Spring 2025 venue by one of the authors registered at the full registration rate. Each workshop registration covers up to two workshop papers by an author. Accepted and presented papers will be published in the IEEE VTC-Spring 2025 Workshop proceedings and submitted to Abstracting and Indexing (A&I) databases. IEEE VTC-Spring 2025 reserves the right to exclude a paper from distribution after the conference, including any Digital Library if the paper is not presented by the author at the conference.
